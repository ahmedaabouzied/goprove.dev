Tracks nil state through your entire program. Nil checks, early returns, field reloads, interface calls — all proven.

Why proving matters

Formal verification was reserved for flight controllers and nuclear reactors. But your Go backend processes payments, routes vehicles, and serves millions of requests. A nil panic doesn't kill anyone — but it loses money, corrupts data, and pages engineers at 3am.

The barrier wasn't that backend code didn't need proving — it was that proving tools required annotations, specifications, or PhDs. Abstract interpretation removes that barrier. Zero annotations. Just run it.
